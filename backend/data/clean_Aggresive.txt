Category:
  - Aggressive
Pre Advice:
  - Invest in high growth assets like stocks and small-cap funds, Use SIP to manage volatility, Avoid emotional decisions based on market fluctuations
High Growth Investments:
  Stocks:
    Description:
      - Stocks represent ownership in companies and offer high return potential but come with high risk and volatility.
    Safe Risk:
      Returns:
        - ~10% – 14%
      Which One:
        - Blue-chip stocks (large companies)
      Examples:
        - Reliance Industries
        - TCS
        - HDFC Bank
      Best For:
        - Stable growth with relatively lower risk in stock market
    Medium Risk:
      Returns:
        - ~14% – 20%
      Which One:
        - Large + Mid-cap stocks
      Examples:
        - Infosys
        - Larsen & Toubro
        - Asian Paints
      Best For:
        - Balanced growth with moderate risk
    High Risky:
      Returns:
        - ~20% – 30%+
      Which One:
        - Mid-cap & emerging companies
      Examples:
        - Emerging sector stocks
        - High growth startups (listed)
      Best For:
        - High returns with high volatility
      Advice:
        - Invest only a portion and research properly
    Strategy:
      Description:
        - Invest in fundamentally strong companies and hold for long term.
      Rules:
        - Do not invest all money in one stock
        - Hold for long term (5+ years)
        - Avoid emotional buying/selling
      Example:
        - Invest ₹20,000 across 4 strong companies instead of one
  Small Cap Funds:
    Description:
      - Small-cap funds invest in smaller companies with high growth potential but very high risk.
    Safe Risk:
      Returns:
        - ~12% – 15%
      Which One:
        - Diversified small-cap funds with strong track record
      Best For:
        - Moderate exposure to high-growth segment
    Medium Risk:
      Returns:
        - ~15% – 20%
      Which One:
        - Actively managed small-cap mutual funds
      Best For:
        - Higher returns with volatility
    High Risky:
      Returns:
        - ~20% – 30%+
      Which One:
        - Aggressive small-cap funds
      Best For:
        - Maximum growth potential
      Advice:
        - Limit allocation to 10–20% of total portfolio
    Strategy:
      Description:
        - Invest through SIP to reduce risk and handle volatility.
      Rules:
        - Do not invest lump sum
        - Stay invested for long term (5–7 years)
        - Be prepared for market ups and downs
      Example:
        - Invest ₹3000/month in a small-cap fund via SIP
  Diversified Portfolio:
    Description:
      - A diversified portfolio spreads investments across different assets to reduce overall risk.
    Allocation:
      Large Cap Percent:
        - 30
        - 40
      Mid Cap Percent:
        - 20
        - 30
      Small Cap Percent:
        - 10
        - 20
      Debt Percent:
        - 10
        - 20
    Which One:
      - Mix of large-cap funds, mid-cap funds, small-cap funds, and some debt instruments
    Strategy:
      Description:
        - Do not depend on a single asset; spread risk across multiple investments.
      Rules:
        - Diversify across sectors and asset types
        - Rebalance portfolio every 6–12 months
        - Reduce exposure to underperforming assets gradually
      Example:
        - Invest ₹40,000 in large-cap, ₹20,000 in mid-cap, ₹10,000 in small-cap, ₹10,000 in debt
    Benefits:
      - Reduces overall investment risk
      - Balances loss in one asset with gain in another
      - Provides more stable long-term returns
Post Advice:
  - Focus on long-term wealth creation (5+ years minimum)
  - Do not panic during market crashes, volatility is normal
  - Invest through SIP to manage market fluctuations
  - Diversify across large-cap, mid-cap, and small-cap investments
  - Limit small-cap exposure to avoid excessive risk
  - Do not invest all money in a single stock or sector
  - Rebalance your portfolio every 6–12 months
  - Avoid emotional decisions like panic selling or FOMO buying
  - Increase investment gradually as your income grows
  - Always keep a small portion in safe assets for stability
