Category:

Pre Advice:
  - Invest in a mix of equity and debt to balance growth and stability, Use SIP for regular investing, Avoid market timing
Hybrid Mutual Funds:
  Description:
    - Hybrid mutual funds invest in both equity (stocks) and debt (bonds) to provide a balance of growth and stability.
  Asset Definition:
    Equity:
      Type:
        - Growth asset
      Examples:
        - Stocks
        - Equity mutual funds
        - Index funds
    Debt:
      Type:
        - Safe asset
      Examples:
        - Fixed Deposit
        - Debt funds
        - Bonds
  Safe Risk:
    Returns:
      - ~7% – 9%
    Which One:
      - Conservative Hybrid Funds
    Best For:
      - Low risk with slightly better returns than FD
  Medium Risk:
    Returns:
      - ~9% – 12%
    Which One:
      - Balanced Advantage Funds, Aggressive Hybrid Funds
    Best For:
      - Balanced growth with moderate risk
  High Risky:
    Returns:
      - ~12% – 14%+
    Which One:
      - Equity-Oriented Hybrid Funds
    Best For:
      - Higher returns with market exposure
    Advice:
      - Invest only if you can handle short-term fluctuations
  Strategy:
    Description:
      - Invest regularly using SIP to reduce market timing risk and build wealth steadily.
    Benefits:
      - Reduces risk compared to pure equity
      - Provides stable and consistent returns
      - Automatic diversification between equity and debt
    Example:
      - Invest ₹2000 per month in a balanced advantage fund for 5 years
Mix Equity Debt:
  Description:
    - Allocate your money between equity and debt to balance risk and return.
  Asset Definition:
    Equity:
      Type:
        - Growth asset
      Examples:
        - Stocks
        - Equity mutual funds
        - Index funds
    Debt:
      Type:
        - Safe asset
      Examples:
        - Fixed Deposit
        - Debt funds
        - Bonds
  Allocation:
    Equity Percent:
      - 50
      - 70
    Debt Percent:
      - 30
      - 50
  Strategy:
    Description:
      - Maintain a balanced portfolio by combining growth assets (equity) and safe assets (debt).
    Rules:
      - Do not invest 100% in equity
      - Rebalance portfolio every 6-12 months
      - Increase equity slowly as risk tolerance improves
    Example:
      - Invest ₹50,000 in equity mutual funds and ₹30,000 in FD or debt funds
  Benefits:
    - Better returns than FD
    - Lower risk than full equity investment
    - Stable portfolio during market fluctuations
Post Advice:
  - Maintain a balance between equity (growth) and debt (stability)
  - Do not over-invest in equity beyond your risk capacity
  - Use SIP for consistent and disciplined investing
  - Avoid trying to time the market
  - Rebalance your portfolio every 6–12 months
  - Keep some portion in safe assets for stability
  - Do not panic during market fluctuations
  - Increase equity exposure gradually as confidence grows
  - Diversify across hybrid funds and direct equity-debt mix
  - Stay invested for medium to long term (3–5 years)
