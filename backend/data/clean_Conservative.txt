Category:
  - Conservative
Pre Advice:
  - Focus on preserving capital, Invest in low-risk assets, Invest in Debt funds
Fixed Deposit:
  Description:
    - Invest in fixed deposits to earn a stable interest income while keeping your money safe.
  Safe Risk:
    Returns:
      - ~6.3% – 6.7% per annum (varies by bank and tenure)
    Which One:
      - SBI, HDFC, ICICI, Axis
    Best For:
      - Stability + No tension
  Medium Risk:
    Returns:
      - ~6.8% – 7.5% per annum (varies by bank and tenure)
    Which One:
      - IDFC First Bank (~7.5%), RBL Bank (~7.3%)
    Best For:
      - Better returns + Safety
  High Risky:
    Returns:
      - ~7.5% – 8% per annum (varies by bank and tenure)
    Which One:
      - Small Finance Banks, Suryoday SFB (~7.9%),Jana SFB (~8%)
    Best For:
      - Higher returns + Willing to take some risk, Advice:Only invest:Max ₹5 lakh (insurance limit safety)
  Strategy:
    Description:
      - Laddering strategy: Invest in multiple fixed deposits with different maturity dates to ensure liquidity and take advantage of varying interest rates.
    Benefits:
      - Provides regular income as deposits mature at different times.
      - Reduces interest rate risk by diversifying across different rates and tenures.
      - Ensures liquidity by having some deposits maturing sooner than others.
    Example:
      - Invest ₹1 lakh in a 1-year FD, ₹1 lakh in a 3-year FD, and ₹1 lakh in a 5-year FD. This way, you have access to funds at different intervals while maximizing returns.
Debt Funds:
  Description:
    - Debt funds invest in bonds and government securities and offer slightly better returns than FD & can handle small ups & downs.
  Safe Risk:
    Returns:
      - ~6.5% – 7%
    Which One:
      - Liquid Funds, Overnight Funds
    Best For:
      - Emergency fund and very short-term parking
  Medium Risk:
    Returns:
      - ~7% – 8%
    Which One:
      - Short Duration Funds, Corporate Bond Funds
    Best For:
      - Better returns than FD with controlled risk
  High Risky:
    Returns:
      - ~8% – 9%
    Which One:
      - Credit Risk Funds
    Best For:
      - Higher return but with default risk
    Advice:
      - Avoid if you are beginner or survival category
  Strategy:
    Description:
      - Use debt funds for flexibility and better post-tax returns compared to FD.
    Benefits:
      - Higher liquidity than FD
      - Better returns in some cases
      - No fixed lock-in
    Example:
      - Keep emergency fund in liquid funds and short-term goals in short duration funds
Low Risk Instruments:
  Description:
    - These are safe investment options backed by government or high-rated institutions.
  Government Bonds:
    Returns:
      - ~6.5% – 7.5%
    Which One:
      - RBI Floating Rate Bonds, Government Securities (G-Sec), Treasury Bills
    Best For:
      - Very safe long-term investment
    Risk:
      - Very low
  Corporate Bonds:
    Returns:
      - ~7.5% – 9%
    Which One:
      - AAA-rated bonds from companies like HDFC, Reliance, PSU bonds
    Best For:
      - Higher returns than FD
    Risk:
      - Low to medium
    Advice:
      - Invest only in AAA-rated bonds
  Ppf Epf:
    Returns:
      - ~7% – 8%
    Which One:
      - PPF (Public Provident Fund), EPF (Employee Provident Fund)
    Best For:
      - Long-term savings with tax benefits
    Risk:
      - Very low
    Lockin:
      - Long-term (5–15 years)
Post Advice:
  - Maintain a balance between safety and moderate returns
  - Do not rely only on fixed deposits, include debt funds for better returns
  - Invest regularly instead of waiting for perfect market timing
  - Diversify your investments across FD, debt funds, and low-risk instruments
  - Avoid high-risk investments like small-cap stocks or derivatives
  - Use short to medium-term debt funds for better flexibility than FD
  - Keep emergency fund separate and easily accessible
  - Avoid locking all money in long-term FD
  - Review your portfolio every 6–12 months
  - Reinvest FD maturity amounts instead of keeping idle cash
  - Choose high-rated (AAA) bonds and safe instruments only
  - Do not chase high returns or trending investments
  - Start small SIPs in low-risk or hybrid funds if comfortable
  - Ensure liquidity is maintained for unexpected needs
  - Gradually increase exposure to better-return assets as confidence grows
